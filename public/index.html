Shape Comparisons


The following experiment is conducted as part of the class CS 279 - Research Topics in Human Computer Interaction, at Harvard University.

In this activity you will be presented with two black boxes:

  • a static one at the top of the screen and
  • a changing one at the bottom of the screen.
Your task is to judge how the area of the box at the bottom compares to the area of the box at the top.

Here's how this works:
  • click the small black circle at the botton of the screen to start
  • if the box at the bottom of the screen is larger than the one at the top, then click the "larger" button;
    if it is smaller, click the "smaller" button
  • to get your next box, click the small black circle that appears at the bottom of the screen
  • keep going with your comparisons until the activity concludes.
It is important to do this as quickly and accurately as possible.

We will start you off with a practice round where you can try this out. The entire activity will take about 10 minutes.
